Flipping the Switch on Fear

You know that feeling . . . you want something to go well, but you’re just not sure you’ll see the outcome you so deeply desire. You’re worried, uncertain, nervous . . . and you tell a friend, husband, or someone about your angst; and what is their response?

They encourage you to calm yourself.

This well-intentioned advice seems logical and practical. But the fact is . . . it does absolutely no good whatsoever because it’s not how we’re wired. The body cannot quickly downgrade from a state of high angst to “calm.” It’s too much of a giant, impractical leap. Amy Cuddy shares the biology around this and asks, “Rather than fruitlessly trying to change the arousal level of our emotional states from high to low, what if we try to change them from negative to positive? From anxiety to excitement?”

For example, as experienced as I am, and as silly as it sounds, I still get anxious when I speak. But I have learned to redirect the anxiety by thinking about “why” I was giving the talk in the first place. As I do this, my eagerness around my “why” and the subject matter grows, and I cannot help to feel increasing excitement (rather than the fear) to share these ideas with others. This little mind game (which I now know was just me focusing on my “why”) enables my anxieties and fears to dissipate so I can truly bring my authentic self to others.

Ladies, when we are focused on our “why” and when that “why” is about others, we will naturally make decisions and take actions that will lead to the results we so deeply desire, and then find ourselves excited about all the good things that can happen, rather than finding ourselves filled with angst.

As we come toward the end of this season, would you try something with me? Today or tomorrow, just take a solid 5-10 minutes to write down all the reasons why you love cabi.

Now think about your excitement to share those things with others. Stay focused on your “why” as you wear those beautiful clothes, do a couple more shows, and propose.

May this final month of the season be filled with opportunities for others to see your cabi excitement!

Committed to you,

Kimberly
